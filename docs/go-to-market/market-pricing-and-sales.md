# Market, Pricing, and Sales

## Market Category

Codex Plugin Doctor sits at the intersection of developer tooling, AI infrastructure, and release quality assurance.

## Entry Wedge

The initial wedge is narrow on purpose:

`Codex package validation for plugin authors and MCP vendors`

This is easier to explain, easier to distribute, and easier to validate than a broad multi-agent control plane at day one.

## Ideal Customer Profiles

### ICP 1: Public Plugin Authors

Small teams or solo builders packaging Codex plugins for external use.

### ICP 2: SaaS Teams Shipping MCP Support

Developer-first companies exposing features through Codex-compatible packages and related MCP infrastructure.

### ICP 3: Internal AI Platform Teams

Organizations standardizing internal plugins and quality gates for agent tooling.

## Market Pain

- broken first installs damage trust
- support teams become the de facto validation layer
- runtime issues are expensive to reproduce
- no clean quality badge exists for Codex package readiness

## Pricing Model

### Open Source Core

Free local CLI with core checks and JSON output.

### Team Plan

Hosted CI reports, historical runs, signed validation artifacts, and shared issue dashboards.

Indicative pricing:

- Starter Team: `$99/month`
- Growth Team: `$299/month`
- Vendor/Platform: `$750+/month`

## Commercial Upsell Ideas

- private rule packs
- signed compliance-style validation reports
- premium support for MCP vendors
- compatibility certification for additional clients

## Distribution Strategy

### Primary Channels

- GitHub Action adoption
- developer communities discussing MCP and plugin tooling
- launch content focused on broken install pain
- sample failing repositories and public reports

### Secondary Channels

- partner content with MCP vendors
- integration guides for Codex package authors
- targeted outreach to teams shipping public plugins

## Sales Motion

### Initial Motion

Product-led growth through CLI adoption and CI integration.

### Follow-On Motion

Founder-led sales to MCP vendors and AI tooling teams.

## Success Metrics

- weekly CLI downloads
- CI workflow installs
- conversion from OSS to paid reporting
- mean time to first validated package
- number of packages validated per team
